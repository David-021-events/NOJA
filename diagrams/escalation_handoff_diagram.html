The Escalation Handoff

Two judgment modes, one accountable network.

Input · decision to be made
§ Signed
Mode 1 — Design-Time Judgment
Accountable Role
e.g. Head of Credit Risk

Policy is signed in advance and governs all instances automatically. The accountable role signs once; the system applies it at scale. Escalation thresholds are defined inside the signed artifact — routing to HITL is itself an act of encoded judgment.

Signed in advance Runs automatically Governs many instances Threshold defined in policy
Routine cases · below threshold

Threshold breached · escalate
Mode 2 — Human-in-the-Loop
Accountable Role
e.g. Senior Underwriter

Live instance routed to a named human. That human decides. Their signature attaches to this instance only — not to the policy that escalated it.

Live routing Named human decides Signature per instance
Output
Human decision · signed to this case

Design-Time is not the lesser mode

A policy signed in a quiet room before any live data touches it is often the more reliable safeguard. HITL is not automatically safer simply because a human is present at the moment of decision.

HITL is not a fallback for failure

It is a legitimate judgment mode for cases the design-time policy explicitly routes to a named human. The escalation threshold is itself an act of signed judgment — part of the design-time artifact, not an afterthought.
